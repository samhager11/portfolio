My Professional Timeline

The Road to Development

After working for a tech startup in New York City and living with a number of developers,
I realized that the one thing holding me back was technical proficiency in web development - not anymore.

  • Aug 2007 - May 2012

    University of North Dakota-
    School of Business & Public Administration

    Accounting and Entrepreneurship

    I first studied Entrepreneurship during my time at the University of North Dakota as I was interested in what it took to start a business from the ground up. Toward the end of my studies, I realized that the Entrepreneurship degree was great in theory but that I needed some hard skills to provide real value for a potential employer. That's when I decided to complete a degree in Accountancy as a solid companion to my Entrepreneurship knowledge. The combination of these two contrasting ways of thinking has been instrumental in my success thus far.

  • May 2011 - Feb 2014

    MAC, Inc. – Bismarck, ND

    Manager of Financial Planning & Analysis

    MAC is an energy solutions provider based in North Dakota. I was responsible for the Accounting and Finance teams and enjoyed the different roles I got to take on in this rapidly expanding company. During my time with the Company I administered the company-wide ERP software system, served as the go-to for all things tech related, and directed the design and implementation of an SEO optimized website while working with one of the foremost SEO specialists in the Nation. My creative side was satisfied through the various proof of concept models I constructed for potential emerging business units as I was solely responsible for their design and implementation.

  • Feb 2014 - June 2015

    Change My World Now - New York City

    Director of Operations

    Change My World Now is an early stage children's social network startup that was pre-funding at the time I joined. The majority of my role was on the fundraising trail, crafting strategic messaging, pitching the investment opportunity, and working closely with prospective investors. We ultimately were able to raise $2.175M in private equity, a difficult feat for a pre-revenue startup. During and after the funding cycle I worked cross-department with the developers to produce and fine-tune assets for potential investors and clients alike. My curiosity eventually got the the best of me and led me to learning web development.

  • Sept 2015 - Jan 2015

    General Assembly - Santa Monica, CA

    Student of Web Development Immersive

    Completing GA's Web Development Immersive was a game changer for me as I am now empowered to create, design, develop and deploy concepts that can be grown into sustainable businesses. During this 12-Week intensive course, I learned the art of coding and covered a wide range of technologies and techniques. We delved into computer science theory, best practicies, workflow, test-driven development, CRUD and RESTful resources, API integration and technologies including:
    • Languages: JavaScript, Ruby, HTML5, CSS3
    • MEAN Stack: MongoDB, ExpressJS, AngularJS, NodeJS
    • Mental Models: Object Oriented Programming, CRUD and RESTful resources, Git Workflow
    • Other: Ruby on Rails, Git/GitHub, Heroku, jQuery, AJAX, API Integration, Bootstrap, Materialize, SemanticUI
